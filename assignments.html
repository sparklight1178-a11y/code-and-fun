TYPES of GRASSLANDS

grasslands have four types:

1.Tropical Grasslands or Savannas

They are located neaar the equator, exhibiting hot temparatures round-year with distinct wet and dry dry seasons.They are characterized by a mix of grasses and individual trees.They support large herbivores predators. They are found in Africa , South America , India and Pakistan.

2.Temperate Grasslands

These are found in regions with varying seasonal temperatures, typically located north of the Tropic of Cancer or South of the tropic of Capricon. They are often broken down into tallgrass, mixed-grass, and shortgrass prairies.bison, pronghorns, and prairie dogs are common inhabitants.

3.Montane or Alpine Grasslands

These are Located at high altitudes across major mountain ranges globally, including the Andes, the Alps, and the Himalayas. they have cold, windy, and often harsh climate. They consist of low-growing plants and grasses.

4.Flooded Grasslands

These are ecosystems saturated with or covered by water for at least part of the year.They support specially adapted aquatic and semi-aquatic grasses and attract a high volume of migratory birds and wetland wildlife. They are found in Florida,USA and South America.

Salient features of grasslands:

They develop in regions with moderate rainfall and hold massive amounts of carbon in their deep, fertile root systems and support major global agriculture.They are among the most endangered biomes due to urban sprawl, overgrazing, and conversion into cropland.

Grazing

Grazing is characteristic of grasslands.Grazing is a method of feeding where herbivores eat low-growing plants like grasses, clover, and algae.

ecological role:

In grasslands, grazing is a foundational ecological process that shapes the entire biome. Because grasses evolved alongside large herbivores, the survival of these ecosystems depends directly on grazing pressure

Threats of imbalance

Overgrazing:

Overgrazing occurs When livestock stay in one place too long, they destroy root systems, compact the soil, and cause erosion, leading to desertification.

Undergrazing:

Under-grazing: Without any grazing, dead grass builds up, chokes out new growth, and reduces plant diversity , leaving the grassland unhealthy and prone to catastrophic fires.
